About Us

Gothelney Hall and its farm are the home of Richard and Victoria Price and their four children. The property was bought three generations ago by Victoriaâ€™s grandfather and was acquired four years ago by Richard and Victoria.

The land on which it stands had a settlement in circa 1026, was named Godelege (Good Meadow) and is referenced in the Doomsday Book. The name subsequently became Gothelney in Anglo Saxon, meaning Open Fields. It has been grazed or farmed for over a thousand years.

Fred Price has taken responsibility for the farm and is in the process of refining and implementing his vision of marrying advanced agricultural techniques with careful husbandry of the natural environment to produce high quality foods.

The Prices are in the midst of a process of renovation of the Hall and its outbuildings, the most recent example being the Stable Block, which is now a spacious and characterful meeting and event venue.
